# Player Guide

Welcome to the table. Campaign Tracker is a shared battle map and combat tracker your
**Game Master (GM)** runs. As a player you mostly read the map and the turn order; your
character appears as a token. This guide covers everything you can do.

## Getting in
- If the GM sent you a **link**, open it. In cloud mode you'll be asked to sign in: enter
  your email and click the **magic link** the app emails you. No password.
- The header badge shows **CLOUD** (a shared, synced game) or **LOCAL** (a single browser).
- Everything updates **live** — when the GM moves a token or reveals a room, you see it
  immediately. No need to refresh.

## Reading the map
- **Move around** — drag to pan, scroll or pinch to zoom, and press **Fit** to recentre.
- **Fog of war** — you only see what your party has **discovered** and what is **lit**.
  Dark or unexplored areas are hidden on purpose; that's your character's limited view,
  not a bug.
- **Whose turn it is** — the active combatant's token has a glowing **gold ring**, and the
  current **round** number is shown in the tracker.
- **Distances** display in **feet or metres** depending on the campaign setting.

## Tools you can use
These live in the toolbar above the map and are safe for anyone to use — they don't change
the game, they just help you see and plan:
- **Reach** — highlights the squares a creature threatens in melee.
- **Range** — click your token first, then turn this on to see how far it can move in a
  turn: **green** = a single move, **gold** = double move, **orange** = run. The range is
  routed **around walls**, so it reflects where you can actually go.
- **Measure** — drag anywhere on the map to measure a distance, without moving anyone.
  Handy for ranged attacks and spells.

## The combat tracker (below the map)
- **Initiative order** — combatants are listed top-to-bottom in turn order; the active one
  is highlighted.
- **Conditions** — your active conditions appear as labelled chips. Timed ones show a
  **rounds remaining** counter that ticks down on your turn.
- **Damage** — the red number on a token is the damage the GM is tracking for it.

## Tips & etiquette
- If you can't see something you expected to, it's probably outside your character's
  **light** or **line of sight** — ask the GM.
- Avoid editing combatants, the map, or the fog unless your GM asks you to; those are the
  GM's tools.
- Roll your dice however your table prefers — the app tracks the encounter, not your rolls.

Happy adventuring!
